APEX FINANCIAL GROUP
PRIVACY POLICY
Version 3.2 | Effective January 15, 2025

1. ACCOUNTABILITY

Apex Financial Group is responsible for personal information under its control and shall designate a Chief Privacy Officer (CPO) who is accountable for the organization's compliance with privacy principles. The CPO shall implement policies and practices to give effect to these principles, establish complaint handling procedures, and train all staff regarding our privacy policies and practices. The CPO's contact information is made available to individuals upon request and is posted on our website.

2. IDENTIFYING PURPOSES

Apex Financial Group shall identify the purposes for which personal information is collected at or before the time the information is collected. We document the purposes for which personal information is collected and provide this documentation to individuals upon request. When personal information that has been collected is to be used for a purpose not previously identified, the new purpose shall be identified prior to use. Unless the new purpose is required by law, the consent of the individual is required before information can be used for that purpose.

3. CONSENT

The knowledge and consent of the individual are required for the collection, use, or disclosure of personal information, except where inappropriate. Apex Financial Group shall make a reasonable effort to ensure that the individual is advised of the purposes for which the information will be used. In obtaining consent, the reasonable expectations of the individual are relevant. Consent shall not be obtained through deception. An individual may withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice. We shall inform the individual of the implications of such withdrawal.

The form of consent may vary depending on the sensitivity of the information. Express consent shall be obtained for the collection, use, or disclosure of sensitive information such as medical records, income, or financial information. Implied consent may be appropriate when the information is less sensitive.

4. LIMITING COLLECTION

The collection of personal information shall be limited to that which is necessary for the purposes identified by Apex Financial Group. Information shall be collected by fair and lawful means. We do not collect personal information indiscriminately and we limit the amount and type of information gathered to what is necessary for the identified purposes.

5. LIMITING USE, DISCLOSURE, AND RETENTION

Personal information shall not be used or disclosed for purposes other than those for which it was collected, except with the consent of the individual or as required by law. Personal information shall be retained only as long as necessary for the fulfilment of those purposes. Apex Financial Group maintains retention schedules and procedures for the destruction, erasure, or anonymization of personal information that is no longer required for its identified purpose. Personal information used to make a decision about an individual shall be retained long enough to allow the individual to access the information after the decision has been made.

6. ACCURACY

Personal information shall be as accurate, complete, and up-to-date as is necessary for the purposes for which it is to be used. Apex Financial Group shall update personal information as necessary to fulfill the identified purposes. We shall not routinely update personal information unless such a process is necessary to fulfill the purposes for which the information was collected. Individuals may challenge the accuracy and completeness of their personal information.

7. SAFEGUARDS

Personal information shall be protected by security safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information. Apex Financial Group employs physical measures (locked filing cabinets, restricted access to offices), organizational measures (security clearances, need-to-know access, confidentiality agreements), and technological measures (encryption, firewalls, passwords, audit trails) to protect personal information against loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification. Our employees are made aware of the importance of maintaining the security of personal information.

8. OPENNESS

Apex Financial Group shall make readily available to individuals specific information about its policies and practices relating to the management of personal information. This includes the name and contact information of the Chief Privacy Officer, the means of gaining access to personal information held by the organization, a description of the types of personal information held and a general account of its use, copies of any brochures or other information that explain our policies and standards, and what personal information is made available to related organizations.

9. INDIVIDUAL ACCESS

Upon request, an individual shall be informed of the existence, use, and disclosure of their personal information and shall be given access to that information. Apex Financial Group shall respond to requests within 30 days and at minimal or no cost to the individual. An individual shall be able to challenge the accuracy and completeness of the information and have it amended as appropriate. When a challenge is not resolved to the satisfaction of the individual, the substance of the unresolved challenge shall be recorded.

An organization shall not give access to personal information if doing so could reasonably be expected to threaten the life or security of another individual, reveal confidential commercial information, or interfere with a law enforcement investigation.

10. CHALLENGING COMPLIANCE

An individual shall be able to address a challenge concerning compliance with the above principles to the designated Chief Privacy Officer of Apex Financial Group. We have established procedures to receive and respond to complaints or inquiries about our policies and practices relating to the handling of personal information. We shall investigate all complaints and take appropriate measures including amending our policies and practices if necessary.

11. BREACH OF SECURITY SAFEGUARDS

Apex Financial Group shall report to the Privacy Commissioner of Canada any breach of security safeguards involving personal information under our control if it is reasonable in the circumstances to believe that the breach creates a real risk of significant harm to an individual. We shall notify affected individuals of any breach that creates a real risk of significant harm. We shall keep records of every breach of security safeguards involving personal information under our control for a period of 24 months. The record shall contain sufficient information to enable the Commissioner to verify compliance.

12. CROSS-BORDER TRANSFERS

When Apex Financial Group transfers personal information to a third party in another jurisdiction for processing, we use contractual or other means to provide a comparable level of protection while the information is being processed by the third party. Individuals shall be notified that their personal information may be transferred outside of Canada and that it may be accessible to law enforcement and national security authorities of that jurisdiction.

13. AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING

Where Apex Financial Group uses automated decision-making processes that have a significant impact on individuals, we provide meaningful information about the logic involved and allow individuals to request human review of automated decisions. We shall ensure that automated systems do not produce discriminatory outcomes.

14. BUSINESS TRANSACTIONS

In the context of prospective or completed business transactions such as mergers, acquisitions, or divestitures, Apex Financial Group may use or disclose personal information without consent where the information is necessary to determine whether to proceed with the transaction and the parties have entered into an agreement requiring the recipient to use or disclose the information solely for purposes related to the transaction.
